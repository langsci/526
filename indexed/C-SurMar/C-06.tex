\scp{\tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai}}{C-06}
\tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai} languages are spoken in areas where
multiple different kinds of marsupials are found: cuscus (\it{Phalangeridae}),
bandicoots (\it{Peramelidae}), as well as tree kangaroos (\it{Dendrolagus}) and wallabies (\it{Macropodiae}).
At l\ili{east} one species of gliding possum also occurs in the \ili{Kei}
Islands, but no term for it is known to be recorded.
See \srf{14-01} for a more detailed overview.
\citet[222]{OsmondPawley2011} reconstruct \tsc{POc} *wakin ‘wallaby’
and point to cognates in SHWNG languages on which basis they reconstruct PEMP *wakin.
Some \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai} languages have marsupial terms
which are derivable from a very similar form **waked.
