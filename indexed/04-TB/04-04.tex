\scp{\tsc{\ili{Lakalei}-Idate}}{04-04}
\ili{As} discussed at the beginning of the previous section,
\ili{Lakalei} and \ili{Idate} may belong within the \tsc{Eastern Timor} subgroup.
Evidence for this would come from *ŋ > \it{n},
lexically specific *R > {\0},
and lexically specific *j > \it{r}.
However, the lexical evidence presented in the tables in the
previous section shows that \tsc{\ili{Lakalei}-Idate}
and \tsc{Eastern Timor} stand apart in both the lexicon
and how the sound correspondences manifest in words which are cognate.
\tsc{\ili{Lakalei}-Idate} is differentiated from \tsc{Eastern Timor}
as it has *j > \it{l} in some words where \tsc{Eastern Timor}
has *j > \it{r},
as well as *R = \it{r} in some words where \tsc{Eastern Timor}
has *R > {\0}.
Examples were given in \trf{04-09} (\prf{tab:04-09})
and \trf{04-10} (\prf{tab:04-10}) respectively.

\ili{Lakalei} and \ili{Idate} can be placed together with each other (and
apart from \tsc{Eastern Timor}) on the basis of shared *b > \it{h}
in all word positions (\trf{04-16},
\prf{tab:04-16}), *j > \it{l} in *qaləjaw > \ili{Lakalei} \it{lelo},
\ili{Idate} \it{lelo} ‘sun’, and *pija > \ili{Lakalei} \it{ila},
\ili{Idate} \it{yila}, as well as at l\ili{east} five lexically specific
innovations, given in \trf{04-17}.
