\chapter{Sula-Buru}\label{ch:SB}
In \crf{ch:CM} (particularly \srf{07-04})
and \crf{ch:Tal} (particularly \srf{08-01}) we showed that \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram}
and \tsc{Taliabu} have innovations that set them apart from \tsc{Sula-Buru}.
In this chapter we show that \tsc{Sula-Buru} itself can be identified as a subgroup.
Within \tsc{Sula-Buru} we can identify a primary division between
\ili{Ambelau} and \tsc{Nuclear Sula-Buru}.
\tsc{Nuclear Sula-Buru} further divides into \tsc{Sula} and \tsc{Buru} nodes.

The sound changes which are shared by all \tsc{Sula-Buru} languages
are given in (1) below.
All these sound changes occur in neighbouring languages to varying
extents, but no neighbouring subgroup shares the same set of sound changes.
This group of changes thus allows us to identify \tsc{Sula-Buru} as separate subgroup.
In addition to these changes,
\tsc{Sula-Buru} has *ñ/*n > \it{n} which is found widely among MP languages.

Examples of \tsc{Sula-Buru} *ə > **e are given in \trf{09-01},
alongside reflexes in three \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} languages
and one \tsc{Taliabu} language.
The \tsc{Sula} node of \tsc{Sula-Buru} has subsequent **e > \it{i} in final syllables.
Additional data showing the reflexes of *ə in \tsc{Sula-Buru}
and \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} include the reflexes of *bətaw ‘sister’
(\trf{07-04}), *kədəŋ ‘stand’ (\trf{07-06}), *baqəRu ‘new’ (\trf{07-11}),
and *təlu ‘three’ (\trf{07-14}).
Additional data for *ə in \tsc{Sula-Buru}
and \tsc{Taliabu} can be found in \srf{08-01-01} (\trf{08-05}
and \trf{08-06}).

Some \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} languages (like \ili{Kamarian}) have *ə > \it{e}
in all positions, some have a split (like \ili{Kaibobo}),\footnote{
		\ili{Kaibobo} mostly
		has *ə > \it{o} /{\gap}(C)V\tsc{[+back]},
		*ə > \it{e} /{\gap}(C)V\tsc{+front},
		and *ə > \it{e},
		\it{o} /{\gap}(C){\#}.}
and some have *ə > \it{o} in all positions (like \ili{Nusalaut}).
To account for all the data in \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} we must reconstruct
Proto-\ili{Ambon}-Seram **ə as the reflex of PMP *ə.
However, alongside **ə \citet[66]{Collins1983} points out that
some instances of PMP *ə shifted to \it{e} in all \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram}
languages; including in languages which otherwise have *ə > \it{o}.
(Examples from \ili{Nusalaut} include *p\tbr{ə}ñu > \it{\tbr{e}nu} ‘turtle’,
*b\tbr{ə}li > \it{alah\tbr{e}ri} ‘buy’,
and *haR\tbr{ə}zan > \it{\tbr{e}lan} ‘ladder’.) \citet{Collins1983}
thus posits a split of *ə > **ə,
**e in \tsc{\ili{Piru} Bay}.

Examples of \tsc{Sula-Buru} *-aw > \it{a} are given in \trf{09-02}.
Additional examples from \ili{Buru} (for which the most data is available)
