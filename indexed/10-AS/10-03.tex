\scp{Languages without full merger of *d/*z/*R/*l/*j}{10-03}
The appearance of the merger of *d/*z/*R/*l/*j is one of the
most distinctive features of \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} languages when
compared with neighbouring subgroups.
However, careful examination of the data shows that a full merger
of *d/*z/*R/*l/*j cannot be assigned to Proto-\ili{Ambon}-Seram.
At best, only the merger of non-final *j/*l > **l can be assigned to Proto-\ili{Ambon}-Seram.
In this section we discuss four branches of \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram}
that do not display the full merger: Boano (\srf{10-03-01}),
\tsc{Atamanu} (\srf{10-03-02}), \tsc{Patakai-Manusela} (\srf{10-03-03}),
\tsc{\ili{Seti}-Benggoi} (\srf{10-03-04}), and \ili{Hoti} (\srf{10-03-05}).

The evidence against the full merger is often quite subtle,
sometimes being evidenced through different conditioning environments for certain
segments requiring that they be kept distinct,
at l\ili{east} in those environments.
We also discuss other issues in the historical phonology of these
languages alongside the reflexes of *d,
*z, *R, *l, and *j.
