\scp{\tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} classification}{10-04}
Parts of these trees follow \citet{Collins1983} uncritically,
with a few differences where subgrouping was proposed on the
basis of splits or mergers that our data show are lexically specific,
or not full splits or mergers.
In particular, the structure of the \tsc{\ili{Piru} Bay}
branch follows \citet{Collins1983} exactly.
The main difference is the placement of Boano as a primary branch
of \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} (rather than within \tsc{East Hoamoal}).
A detailed assessment adjusting for current views of PMP phonology
is yet to be done.

No systematic attempt has been made here to reconcile language
names used by Collins with those in other sources.
Some authors refer to “dialects” of what others might consider
distinct languages on the basis of different historical sound
correspondences and severely limited intelligibility,
while other authors refer to speech varieties by the name of
the village in which their data was collected.
Much work remains to be done,
both descriptively and comparatively.

\ili{Salas} [sgu] is a language of eastern Seram that is linked to
\tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} languages on the basis of lexical similarity by \citet{LoskiLoski1989}.
However, no data is available for this language to assess whether
this classification finds support from the historical phonology,
and if so, where within \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} it might belong.
Due to the lack of clarity we have not connected it to other branches of \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram}.
