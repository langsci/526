\sscp{Hoti}{10-03-05}
\ili{Hoti} is a (probably extinct) language of north\ili{east} Seram spoken
in the village of the same name.
It is known from a word list in \citet{Ludeking1868}.
Based on this data \citet{Stresemann1927} included \ili{Hoti} in his Sub-Seran subgroup.
\citet[117]{Collins1982c} writes ‚Äú\ili{Hoti}
and \ili{Liambata}, as Stresemann‚Äôs record of them can be interpreted,
are \ili{Seti} languages [{\ldots}]‚Äù.
However, the data which is available is very different from \tsc{Seti}
(\srf{10-03-04}) and \ili{Hoti} is clearly a separate language based on different sound
correspondences and the lexicon.
\ili{Hoti} has a large number of unconditioned splits which makes classifying it difficult.

Our discussion is based primarily on the data as presented by
\citeauthor{Stresemann1927}‚Äôs \citeyear{Stresemann1927} interpretation of \citet{Ludeking1868}.
Data taken directly from \citet{Ludeking1868} are indicated.
We interpret ‚Äπph‚Ä∫ as a bilabial fricative […∏].
PAMS reconstructions in this section follow Stresemann‚Äôs transcription.

We place \ili{Hoti} in \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram},
primarily based on the merger of *n/*≈ã,
combined with the lack of any positive evidence for grouping
it with any branch of \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai}.
We further identify it as a primary branch of \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram}
due to the fact that *R does not fully merge with *l.

\ili{Hoti} appears to merge *n/*√±/*≈ã,
with a subsequent split to \it{n, l, r}.
The outcome of the split is partially conditioned by the quality
of the following vowel.
Before a high vowel *n/*√±/*≈ã > \it{r},
with only one instance of *≈ã > \it{l} in this environment.
Examples are given in \qf{10-71}.

While we can posit a complete merger of *d/*l (which further
possibly merge with *n/*≈ã),
we can only posit a partial merger of *R with these segments.
Thus, if \ili{Hoti} is indeed an \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} language,
it does not attest the merger of *R/*l seen in all other branches except Boano (\srf{10-03-01}).

We note also that the (partial) conditioning of *d/*l/*n/*≈ã >
\it{l, r} according to the height of adjacent vowels is a pattern seen
only in \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} languages
and is not found in \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai}.

In the final syllable the most common reflex of *…ô is \it{a} (six examples).
There are also four examples of final syllable *…ô > \it{o},
and *…ô > \it{i}.
The larger number of  examples of *…ô > \it{a} in final syllables
may point to a connection with \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai}
(\crf{ch:STB}) in which *…ô > \it{a} in final syllables is one
of the defining changes.
Note, however, that in the two words with cognates in \ili{Bobot}
and \ili{Masiwang} -- the members \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai} closest
to \ili{Hoti} -- the reflexes of schwa in final syllables do not agree.
