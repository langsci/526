\sscp{\tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} and \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai}}{07-03-03}
\tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram}, \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai}, and \ili{Banda} have
a merger of *d/*z in most words.
The outcome in Proto-Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai was
probably **d, as \ili{Yamdena} has *d > \it{r,
d} and *z > \it{d}.
However, \ili{Banda} has *d/*z > \it{r},
and the outcome in \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} is more diverse: \tsc{Nuclear
\ili{Ambon}-Seram} has *d/*z > **l (reflexes as \it{r} or \it{d} are due to later changes; \srf{10-01}),
%\footnote{
		%Reflexes of *d/*z in \tsc{Nuclear
		%\ili{Ambon}-Seram} as \it{r} or \it{d} are due to later changes
		%of **l (\srf{10-01}).}
\tsc{Patakai-Manusela} has *d/*z > **h,
and \tsc{Atamanu} has *d/*z > **r.
All these \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} reflexes can probably be explained
by positing *d/*z > **r for Proto-\ili{Ambon}-Seram.

While it is not unlikely that **r in \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram}
and \ili{Banda} went through intermediate **d,
it is not certain.
The merger of *d/*z in these branches could have
had a different quality,
such as [z] and/or [ʤ],
in which case the merger of *d/*z in \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram},
\ili{Banda}, and \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai} would not be a shared innovation.
In any case, if merger of *d/*z occurred once in a common ancestor
of \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram}, \ili{Banda} and \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai}, the evidence indicates
that Proto-\ili{Ambon}-Seram subsequently underwent a change independent of \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai}.
See \srf{13-04} for more discussion.

Examples of the merger are given in \trf{07-07}.
Note that many \tsc{Nuclear \ili{Ambon}-Seram} languages have subsequent
changes to *d/*z > (**r >) **l conditioned by the adjacent vowels (see \srf{10-01}).
Some members of \tsc{Tanimbar-Bomberai} have conditioned reflexes
of *d when the following syllable contains /r/.
That is, in the environment /{\gap}Vr, e.g.
*d in *duRi ‘thorn,
bone’ has different reflexes to *d in *duha ‘two’(see \srf{11-05-01}).
Most languages of \tsc{Sula-Buru} have *z/*y > **y,
but different reflexes of *d,
meaning we cannot posit merger of *d/*z for \tsc{Sula-Buru}.

\tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram}, \ili{Banda}, and nearly all \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai}
languages also usually have lenition of *p > **f/**ɸ,
often with subsequent further lenition to \it{h, {\0}}.
However, \ili{Kur} retains *p unchanged in all positions (\srf{11-04}),
and there are sporadic retentions of *p = \it{p} in \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram}.
Examples of *p = \it{p} in \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} include: *\tbr{p}əñu > \ili{Alune}
\it{\tbr{p}one} ‘(land) tortoise’, (cf.
\ili{Alune} \it{benu} ‘sea turtle’),
\ili{Larike} \it{\tbr{p}one{\tl}\tbr{p}one} ‘(land) tortoise’,
as well as *\tbr{p}a- \tsc{causative} > South \ili{Nuaulu} \it{a\tbr{p}a-,
a\tbr{p}u}; \ili{Asilulu} \it{\tbr{p}a-, a-},
and \ili{Larike} \it{\tbr{p}a-, a-}.

\ili{Yamdena} and \ili{Banda} also retain *p unchanged word finally.
\ili{Yamdena} examples include: *mali\tbr{p} > \it{mali\tbr{p}} ‘laugh’
and *ma-qudi\tbr{p} > \it{n-mori\tbr{p}} ‘live’. The single
\ili{Banda} example in the available data is *qatə\tbr{p} > \it{ato\tbr{p}} ‘thatch, roof’.
\ili{Banda} has initial and medial *p > {\0}.
This could have passed through intermediate **f/**ɸ in which
case it would be a change shared with \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram}
and most of \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai},
but it could have had an alternate intermediate stage,
such as [ʔ], in which case the reflexes of *p would not be shared
between \ili{Banda} and other languages of the region.

Weakening of *p is an areal change which also occurs
in \tsc{Aru}, \tsc{Timor-Babar}, and SHWNG.
Within \tsc{Sula-Buru} only \ili{Ambelau} has *p > \it{f},
with other \tsc{Sula-Buru} languages retaining *p = \it{p}.
