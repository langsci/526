\scp{Possibilities of shared innovations in the phonology}{13-01}
In the following sections we look at various issues in the historical
phonologies that might be used to link Wallacean subgroups together
at a higher level.
The summary tables in the following sections are usually at the
level of the Wallacean subgroups we have identified.
Within these subgroups there may be some nodes that reflect the
retention or value given for the whole subgroup,
and others that reflect a subsequent secondary development or loss.
Further study which provides a more granular listing that includes
the subnodes of each subgroup might give other useful insights
into the history of these subnodes.
The detailed reflexes for subnodes are usually given in the summary
classifications at the end of each chapter.
