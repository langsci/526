\scp{Nasal-consonant clusters}{12-04}
Both \citet{Stresemann1927}
and \citet[133f]{Collins1986} noticed the behaviour of historical
*NC clusters in \ili{Banda} was different from \tsc{\ili{Ambon}-Seram} languages
(\citeauthor{Stresemann1927}), and from \ili{Geser} and \ili{Watubela} (\citeauthor{Collins1986}).
\ili{Banda} retains both the nasal and the stop.
\ili{Banda} reflexes are shown in \qf{12-10} from the small number
of PMP words with an *NC cluster with reflexes in \ili{Banda}.

Notice also the root-initial NC clusters that appear from inflections
on the verbs for ‘pay’
and ‘plant’ in \trf{12-01}.
Similar NC inflectional patterns are found in many other verbs,
listed in \qf{12-11}, many of which have no known PMP etyma.

While uninflected reflexes are sparse,
nevertheless the patterns shown here are different from \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai},
and particularly from the \tsc{Eastern Islands} node,
in which *mp/*mb > **mb,
but *ma-p > **map,
and *ma-b > **mab,
the latter two not merging,
and preserving all three segments (see \srf{13-01-02}).
