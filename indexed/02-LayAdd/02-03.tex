\scp{Complexity in the languages}{02-03}
In addition to normal language diversification resulting from
Austronesian dispersal, there were also widespread changes
that happened in the typology,
structures, and semantics due to contact with various Papuan
languages already in place (\srf{02-03-01}).
Numerous sociolinguistic factors also combined to result in a
plethora of synonyms in the region (\srf{02-03-02}).
Lexical borrowing can be fairly trivial.
Structural and typological changes from contact are quite significant.
