\sscp{\tsc{Flores} as a node}{03-02-02}
There is some weak evidence for a \tsc{Flores} node containing
\ili{Komodo}, and all the languages of Flores
and the \ili{Solor} archipelago.
This evidence comes from merger of *z/*j in a subset of words.
We acknowledge that the basis for \tsc{Flores} is quite weak
and it may be better to treat shared merger of *z/*j in these
words as a result of contact and/or diffusion.
There are three well defined nodes within Flores: \tsc{West Flores},
\tsc{\ili{Central} Flores}, and \tsc{Flores-Lembata}.
There is also some weak evidence for placing \tsc{West Flores}
and \tsc{\ili{Central} Flores} in a single node.

Eleven words which display the merger of *z/*j are given in \trf{03-26}
and \trf{03-27}, along with reflexes from \ili{Bima}
and \ili{Havu} to show that these other languages do not participate in the merger.
Irregular/unexpected reflexes are shaded.
(These irregular reflexes further weaken the evidence for \tsc{Flores}.)
The different reflexes of this merger are summarised in \qf{03-01} below.

There are two likely possibilities for the outcome of the merger
of *z/*j in \tsc{Flores}.
They may have merged to a voiced alveolar fricative **z,
or to a voiced coronal affricate (with fricative allophones) **ʤ or **ʣ.

Nearly all branches of \tsc{Flores} have a subsequent merger
of PMP *d with *z/*j.
However, the merger is not complete in \tsc{West Flores},
and does not take place in \ili{Lio}, which keeps the two sets distinct
(*z/*j > \it{ʤ}, *d > \it{r}).
This means the merger cannot be posited for \tsc{\ili{Central} Flores}.
