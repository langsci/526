\scp{Partial merger of PMP apicals}{10-01}
While there is a strong appearance of a merger of PMP apicals
*d/*z/*R/*l/*j > **l in \tsc{Ambon-Seram},
nevertheless careful scrutiny shows it is not a full merger at
the level of the whole subgroup,
and different nodes reflect varying combinations and conditioned reflexes.
Thus we cannot posit the full merger at the level of \tsc{Ambon-Seram}.
Understanding \tsc{Ambon-Seram} requires unravelling the partially
overlapping mergers of the PMP apicals.
Nonetheless, in many languages we can posit a full merger of *d/*z/*R/*l/*j > **l.
We place languages which share the complete merger within a single
branch; \tsc{Ambon-West Seram}.

\newpage
The merger is obscured by complex splits in
some languages of **l > \it{l,
r, d} as conditioned by adjacent vowels.
These splits are discussed in detail by \citet[23--33]{Stresemann1927}.
He identifies five main types of languages according to the environment(s)
which determine the split.
These are summarised in \trf{10-01}.

of **l are shaded.

Word medially, most languages also have **l = \it{l} between non-high vowels.
There are two main exceptions.
Firstly, western varieties of Alune have **l > \it{r} before \it{a} and \it{o}.
Examples of **l /V{\gap}\it{a,o} are given in \trf{10-03}.
(Additional examples of West Alune *l > \it{r} /V{\gap}a,o are
given in \trf{10-07}.) Secondly,
the variety of \tsc{Kamarian} from the village of the same name
has **l > \it{r} before or after \it{e}.
 Other varieties of \tsc{Kamarian} (Tihulale,
and Rumakai) have **l > \it{l} adjacent to \it{e}.
Examples of **l adjacent to \it{e} are given in \trf{10-04}.
Apart from these languages,
members of \tsc{East Littoral} (Sepa,
Tamilou, Tehoru, Teluti) have **l > **y in certain environments
(see \srf{10-01-02} for fuller discussion)
and certain varieties of Wemale have **l > \it{l},
{\0} between identical vowels.
These changes occurred after *d/*z/*R/*l/*j > **l.

Most examples of **l > \it{r} in \tsc{Ambon-Seram} languages
are conditioned by an adjacent high vowel.
Paulohi, Sepa, Nusalaut, Amahai,
\tsc{Kamarian}, Kaibobo, and Haruku all have **l > \it{r} before high vowels.
Similarly, Larike and western varieties of Alune have **l > \it{d} before high vowels.
Examples word initially are given in \trf{10-05},
and word-medial examples in \trf{10-06}.
Note that \citeauthor{Stresemann1918} (\citeyear[4]{Stresemann1918}, \citeyear[24]{Stresemann1927})
is explicit that Paulohi \it{r} is uvular, not alveolar.
