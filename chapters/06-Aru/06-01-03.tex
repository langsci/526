\sscp{Initial glide insertion}{06-01-03}
Another change which can be assigned to the level of Proto-Aru
is sporadic insertion of initial glides before vowel initial
words (including words which became vowel initial due to loss
of PMP *q and *h), shown in \trf{06-09}.
In most cases the glide inserted is palatal *y,
but *w is often inserted when the word contains *u.

Similar insertions are fairly widespread in the region (see \srf{13-02-02}),
meaning that it constitutes only weak subgrouping evidence.
Furthermore, in \tsc{Aru} glide insertion is sporadic
and does not affect all vowel initial words.
Nonetheless, there are several words for which all reflexes attest
an initial glide, thus allowing reconstruction of the glide to Proto-Aru
for those etyma.
It thus can be taken as supporting evidence for the validity of Proto-Aru.
Three examples are *ijuŋ > **y[ui]Run- ‘nose’,
*ikuR > **y[ui]kuR- ‘tail’,
and *qaluR > **aluR > **waluR ‘deep sea’,
shown in \trf{06-09}.

After glide insertion at the level of Proto-Aru there appears
to have been additional rounds of glide insertion.
One had occurred by the time of Proto-South Aru (see \srf{06-02}),
leading \citet{Nivens2017} to reconstruct an unambiguous glide
for Proto-South Aru in some words,
as seen in *hikan > **(y)ika ‘fish’ > Proto-South Aru **yika
‘fish’, *udu > **(w)uru > **wuru ‘grass,
weeds’, and *quzan > **(w)uyan > **wuyan ‘rain’,
shown in \trf{06-09}.
An additional round of glide insertion also
affected Lola, Dobel, Koba, and Lorang.
This last round of glide insertion occurred after the fortition
of glides previously inserted in Dobel,
Koba, and Lorang, thus reintroducing word-initial glides into these languages.

The data regarding glide insertion in the \tsc{Aru} languages
is probably best explained by positing that it was a change in
progress which had begun at the level of Proto-Aru
and continued to varying degrees in some,
but not all, groups after the break-up of Proto-Aru.
