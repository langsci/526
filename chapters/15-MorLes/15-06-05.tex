\sscp{Secondary Papuan migrations from Timor}{15-06-05}
As mentioned in \srf{02-01},
there are groups speaking Papuan languages on Alor
and Pantar who see their origins as autochthonous to those islands.
They say they have always been there.
There are other groups on Alor
and Pantar who trace their origins to a migration from the Timor mainland.
As also mentioned in \srf{02-01},
speakers of the Papuan language of Oirata spoken on Kisar trace
their origins to a migration from the Timor mainland.
This possibly happened in historical times.
