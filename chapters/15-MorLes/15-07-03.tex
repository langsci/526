\sscp{To include in word lists and glossaries}{15-07-03}
This study has also identified a number of words that would be
useful to include in future word list collections or glossaries in the region.
The list in appendix \ref{ch:ComWor} is a 494-item word list which
is designed to target concepts which are particularly helpful
from a comparative perspective.
It could be used across much of Wallacea
and is likely to yield more data of comparative usefulness in
the region than a standard Swadesh list alone.
(Please consider sending copies to the authors for use in future
comparative works.) Of this list,
313 items (italicised below) have been identified as essential/core
items that should be collected for a language when time is limited
or the situation does not allow collection of all 494 items.

This list, with Indonesian equivalents, and notes on elicitation
is given in appendix \ref{ch:ComWor},
and versions of the list formatted for printing
and for use in the field can be downloaded from \textcolor{red}{\tbf{XXX}}.
Some of these concepts are more useful in limited parts of Wallacea
(such as \tsc{Bima-Lembata}, \tsc{Timor-Babar}, and \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai}; or \tsc{Taliabu},
\tsc{Sula-Buru}, \tsc{Ambon-Seram}; or \tsc{Seram-Tanimbar-Bomberai}
and \tsc{Aru}).\footnote{
		Some of these might be difficult to
		obtain reliably in a survey situation,
		or with limited proficiency in the language of elicitation,
		or before relationships are built.
		For some items (e.g.
		foxtail millet), pictures can be quite useful,
		since very few people in eastern Indonesia recognise the official language term.
		While electronic pictures are useful,
		in remote areas one can go for days without recharging a device,
		so quality printouts can be helpful.}
